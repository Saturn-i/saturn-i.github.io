Why an encyclopedia?

Time for a pop-quiz.
A screw, an engine, a sorting algorithm, and a computer... what do they each have in common? The answer is: they are all technologies. Admittedly, some are more primitive than others - but with them we can enhance our quality of life, and build newer and better technologies with greater ease and efficency. If a computer program is an invisible, virtual machine, then algorithms are the discrete parts - the digital nuts and bolts - which allow that machine to function. Unfortunately, the solutions to every problem we come across are not all obvious. This is where an encyclopedia comes to the rescue.
Imagine living in a world with no technology. Now, your job is to build a Boeing 747 wide-body commercial jet airliner. Your tools going into this project are whatever you can find lying around and whatever you can slap together. You will have to literally re-invent the wheel, along with everything else. I'm sure you will quickly see what a herculean task that is. It's also really stupid (sorry Hercules) when you don't live in such a world - and yet it is more or less the standard operating procedure for many inexperianced programmers. Thankfully, you and I know better than that, and that's why I've compiled a library of algorithms here that we can use as a reference to accomplish our complex programmatic tasks quickly, easily, and hopefully without "accomplishing" a headache along the way. Happy computing! :)
